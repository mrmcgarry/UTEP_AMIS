The Advanced Measurement Instrument and Services (AMIS) project was funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF) and began in April 2015. The AMIS system will achieve flow-granularity network measurement, sustain scalable line rate, meet evolving measurement objectives and derive knowledge for network advancement. The scalable hardware architecture and software-defined measurement framework are key advantages. The main tasks of the project are:

  1. Prototype the Advanced Measurement Instrument and Services (AMIS)
  2. Deploy and Operate AMIS on International R&E Networks
  3. Preserve Privacy of Flow Information and Analyze Traffic Patterns for Understanding Data-Driven Science Discovery
  4. Guide Network Management and Optimization using Flow Analysis Results

The AMIS team consists of faculty and students from the University of Massachussetts, Lowell (UML) , the University of Massachussetts, Boston (UMB) , the University of Kentucky (UKY), , and the University of Texas, El Paso (UTEP) . The Project Team page contains more detailed information on project team members.


A prototype of the AMIS system is currently under development. The prototype development is partitioned among participanting institutions as follows:

  • U Mass - Lowell : Network measurment instrument
  • U Mass - Boston : Data privacy module
  • U Kentucky : Data processing module
  • U Texas - El Paso : User interface and visual analytics
